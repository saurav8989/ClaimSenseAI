========================================================================
                  CLAIMSENSE AI - CLINICAL PROTOCOLS
========================================================================

This document acts as the definitive source of truth for the clinical rules
and Standard Treatment Protocols (STPs) implemented in ClaimSense AI. These
rules are directly based on the Nepalese national Standard Treatment Protocols.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
1. MEDICAL CODING & TERMINOLOGY DICTIONARY
------------------------------------------------------------------------

The system will recognize and support the following mappings:

*   DIAGNOSES (ICD-11 Prefix & Family Codes)
    - [1F4]     Malaria (covers Plasmodium vivax, falciparum, mixed, etc.)
    - [KA64.2]  Congenital malaria
    - [BA00]    Essential Hypertension
    - [5A11]    Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus
    - [1D2]     Dengue Fever
    - [CA40]    Pneumonia

*   DIAGNOSTIC TESTS (Mock Codes)
    - [TEST-MAL-RDT]    Malaria Rapid Diagnostic Test (RDT)
    - [TEST-MAL-MIC]    Malaria Blood Smear / Microscopy
    - [TEST-BP-CHECK]   Blood Pressure Monitoring
    - [TEST-ECG]        Electrocardiogram (ECG)
    - [TEST-RENAL]      Serum Urea & Creatinine
    - [TEST-UA]         Urine Analysis (Dipstick/Microscopic)
    - [TEST-LIPID]      Lipid Profile
    - [TEST-GLU-FPG]    Fasting Plasma Glucose (FPG)
    - [TEST-GLU-PPG]    2-Hour Post-Prandial Glucose (PPG)
    - [TEST-GLU-RBS]    Random Blood Sugar (RBS)
    - [TEST-GLU-HBA1C]  Glycated Hemoglobin (HbA1c)
    - [TEST-UA-PROT]    Urine Protein (Nephropathy check)
    - [TEST-UA-KET]     Urine Ketone (for hyperglycemia check)
    - [TEST-DEN-RDT]    Dengue Rapid Diagnostic Test (Antigen/Antibody)
    - [TEST-CBC]        Complete Blood Count (for Platelet and Hematocrit)
    - [TEST-LFT]        Liver Function Test (AST/ALT)
    - [TEST-RR-CHECK]   Respiratory Rate (RR) check
    - [TEST-LUNG-AUS]   Chest Auscultation (Crepitations/Crackles/Wheeze)

*   TREATMENTS & MEDICATIONS (Mock CPT / RxNorm Codes)
    - [MED-CQ-150]      Chloroquine 150mg base tablet
    - [MED-PQ-7.5]      Primaquine 7.5mg tablet
    - [MED-PQ-2.5]      Primaquine 2.5mg tablet (for infants 6 months - 1 year)
    - [MED-AL-ACT]      Artemether (20mg) + Lumefantrine (120mg) (AL)
    - [MED-DHAP]        Dihydroartemisinin + Piperaquine (DHAP) (Second-line)
    - [MED-ART-INJ]     Inj Artesunate (IV/IM) (Severe/Pre-referral)
    - [MED-AML-5]       Amlodipine 5mg tablet
    - [MED-AML-10]      Amlodipine 10mg tablet (Severe emergency stat)
    - [MED-LOS-25]      Losartan 25mg tablet
    - [MED-LOS-50]      Losartan 50mg tablet (Severe emergency stat)
    - [MED-MET-500]     Metformin 500mg tablet
    - [MED-GLI-1]       Glimepiride 1mg tablet (For lean & thin patients)
    - [MED-PAR-500]     Paracetamol 500mg tablet
    - [MED-ORS]         Oral Rehydration Salts (ORS)
    - [MED-AMOX-500]    Amoxicillin 500mg tablet
    - [MED-AZITH-500]   Azithromycin 500mg tablet
    - [MED-DOXY-100]    Doxycycline 100mg tablet
    - [MED-SAL-INH]     Salbutamol Inhaler (or tablets)
    - [MED-IBU-400]     Ibuprofen 400mg tablet (NSAID - Contraindicated for Dengue)

2. STANDARD TREATMENT PROTOCOL (STP) RULES
------------------------------------------------------------------------

Each claim's chronological `carePathway` must follow these rules:

---

### PROTOCOL 1: Malaria Protocol (Uncomplicated & Severe)
*   **Target Diagnosis**: 1F4, KA64.2 (Malaria / Congenital Malaria)
*   **Rule 1.1: Diagnostic Before Treatment (Mandatory)**
    - *Condition*: Prescribing antimalarials (CQ, PQ, AL, DHAP, or Artesunate) requires prior diagnostic confirmation via RDT or Microscopy (`TEST-MAL-RDT` or `TEST-MAL-MIC`).
    - *Deviation*: Missing diagnostic confirmation before anti-malarial therapy.
    - *Severity*: CRITICAL (Penalty: 40 points)
*   **Rule 1.2: Therapy Alignment (Vivax vs. Falciparum)**
    - *Condition*: 
      - Vivax Malaria: Must include Chloroquine (`MED-CQ-150`) for 3 days and Primaquine (`MED-PQ-7.5` or `MED-PQ-2.5`) for 14 days.
      - Falciparum / Mixed Malaria: Must include Artemether-Lumefantrine (`MED-AL-ACT`) for 3 days and single-dose Primaquine (`MED-PQ-7.5`) on Day 1.
    - *Deviation*: Incorrect first-line therapy or missing partner drug for radical cure.
    - *Severity*: MAJOR (Penalty: 30 points)
*   **Rule 1.3: Primaquine Pregnancy/Lactation Contraindication**
    - *Condition*: Primaquine is strictly contraindicated in pregnant women, infants under 6 months, and lactating mothers of infants under 6 months. For Vivax in pregnancy, CQ weekly chemoprophylaxis is indicated.
    - *Deviation*: Primaquine prescribed to contraindicated pregnant/lactating/infant patient.
    - *Severity*: CRITICAL (Penalty: 50 points)
*   **Rule 1.4: Severe Malaria Pre-referral**
    - *Condition*: Cases presenting severe signs (coma, prostration, multiple convulsions, respiratory distress/ARDS, shock, bleeding, jaundice, renal impairment) must be administered a pre-referral dose of Inj Artesunate (`MED-ART-INJ`) and immediately referred to a higher center (`REFER`).
    - *Deviation*: Severe malaria clinical signs present but missing Inj Artesunate or missing referral.
    - *Severity*: CRITICAL (Penalty: 40 points)
*   **Rule 1.5: Second-Line (DHAP) Restriction**
    - *Condition*: Dihydroartemisinin-Piperaquine (`MED-DHAP`) is only indicated for drug intolerance, treatment failure (recrudescence within 28 days), or CQ-resistant imported vivax. It requires a documented referral to a higher center.
    - *Deviation*: Second-line DHAP prescribed without valid reason or without referral.
    - *Severity*: MAJOR (Penalty: 25 points)

---

### PROTOCOL 2: Essential Hypertension Protocol
*   **Target Diagnosis**: BA00 (Hypertension)
*   **Rule 2.1: BP Check Confirmation (Mandatory)**
    - *Condition*: Claim must contain a `TEST-BP-CHECK` step showing clinical readings.
    - *Deviation*: Prescribing anti-hypertensive without BP record.
    - *Severity*: CRITICAL (Penalty: 40 points)
*   **Rule 2.2: First-Line Monotherapy**
    - *Condition*: Asymptomatic Stage 1 (140-159 / 90-99 mmHg) or Stage 2 (>=160/100 mmHg) must be initiated with low-dose Amlodipine 5mg (`MED-AML-5`) or Losartan 25mg (`MED-LOS-25`) and scheduled for follow-up referral.
    - *Deviation*: Non-approved anti-hypertensive drug used, or incorrect starting dosage.
    - *Severity*: MAJOR (Penalty: 20 points)
*   **Rule 2.3: Hypertensive Emergency / Urgency Referral**
    - *Condition*: If BP is severe (>=180/120 mmHg) or target organ damage is present (stroke, altered mental status, retinopathy, chest pain/ACS, heart failure, renal failure), doctor must administer stat Amlodipine 10mg (`MED-AML-10`) or Losartan 50mg (`MED-LOS-50`) and issue an immediate referral (`REFER`).
    - *Deviation*: Severe hypertension/crisis present without emergency stat dosing or without referral.
    - *Severity*: MAJOR (Penalty: 35 points)

---

### PROTOCOL 3: Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus Protocol
*   **Target Diagnosis**: 5A11 (Type 2 Diabetes)
*   **Rule 3.1: Glycemic Testing (Mandatory)**
    - *Condition*: Prescribing antidiabetic meds (`MED-MET-500` or `MED-GLI-1`) requires prior glycemic testing (`TEST-GLU-FPG`, `TEST-GLU-PPG`, `TEST-GLU-RBS`, or `TEST-GLU-HBA1C`).
    - *Deviation*: Missing glycemic diagnostic verification.
    - *Severity*: CRITICAL (Penalty: 40 points)
*   **Rule 3.2: First-Line Selection & Patient Alignment**
    - *Condition*: The primary therapeutic must be Metformin 500mg (`MED-MET-500`) unless the patient is "lean and thin", in which case Glimepiride 1mg (`MED-GLI-1`) is approved.
    - *Deviation*: Metformin bypassed or Glimepiride prescribed inappropriately.
    - *Severity*: MAJOR (Penalty: 25 points)
*   **Rule 3.3: Preliminary Assessment & Complication Referral**
    - *Condition*: Diabetes management requires assessments for BP, BMI, feet checks, oral cavity, and urine protein. All newly diagnosed patients must be referred (`REFER`) for further evaluation.
    - *Deviation*: Newly diagnosed diabetes claim lacks recorded referral.
    - *Severity*: MINOR (Penalty: 15 points)

---

### PROTOCOL 4: Dengue Fever Protocol
*   **Target Diagnosis**: 1D2 (Dengue Fever)
*   **Rule 4.1: Confirmatory Test (Mandatory)**
    - *Condition*: Dengue diagnosis requires positive Antigen/Antibody confirmation via Dengue RDT (`TEST-DEN-RDT`).
    - *Deviation*: Dengue claim submitted without confirmatory laboratory testing.
    - *Severity*: CRITICAL (Penalty: 35 points)
*   **Rule 4.2: NSAID / Steroid / Antibiotic Contraindication**
    - *Condition*: Claim must not prescribe NSAIDs like Ibuprofen (`MED-IBU-400`), Aspirin, steroids, or antibiotics due to risk of hemorrhagic complications or inappropriate treatment.
    - *Deviation*: Prescribed contraindicated drug (NSAID, steroid, antibiotic) in Dengue.
    - *Severity*: CRITICAL (Penalty: 50 points)
*   **Rule 4.3: Platelet / Hematocrit Monitoring**
    - *Condition*: Dengue management requires a Complete Blood Count (`TEST-CBC`) to monitor platelet counts and hematocrit.
    - *Deviation*: Missing platelet/hematocrit monitoring for Dengue.
    - *Severity*: MAJOR (Penalty: 25 points)
*   **Rule 4.4: Supportive Therapy & Hospital Referral**
    - *Condition*: Supportive therapy is limited to Paracetamol (`MED-PAR-500`) and ORS (`MED-ORS`). All cases must be referred (`REFER`) to a hospital.
    - *Deviation*: Missing referral for suspected/confirmed Dengue case.
    - *Severity*: MAJOR (Penalty: 20 points)

---

### PROTOCOL 5: Pneumonia Protocol
*   **Target Diagnosis**: CA40 (Pneumonia)
*   **Rule 5.1: First-Line Antibiotic Alignment**
    - *Condition*: First-line therapy must consist of Amoxicillin 500mg TDS (`MED-AMOX-500`), Azithromycin 500mg OD (`MED-AZITH-500`), or Doxycycline 100mg BD (`MED-DOXY-100`).
    - *Deviation*: Primary diagnosis Pneumonia treated with non-approved or restricted antibiotics.
    - *Severity*: MAJOR (Penalty: 25 points)
*   **Rule 5.2: Doxycycline Age & Pregnancy Contraindication**
    - *Condition*: Doxycycline (`MED-DOXY-100`) must NOT be prescribed in children under 12 years of age or in pregnant/lactating women.
    - *Deviation*: Doxycycline prescribed to child under 12 or pregnant/lactating woman.
    - *Severity*: CRITICAL (Penalty: 45 points)
*   **Rule 5.3: Severe Case Referral**
    - *Condition*: Pneumonia cases presenting severe dyspnea, cyanosis, stridor, or showing no improvement after 3 days must be immediately referred (`REFER`) to a higher center.
    - *Deviation*: Severe pneumonia clinical markers present but missing referral.
    - *Severity*: MAJOR (Penalty: 30 points)

------------------------------------------------------------------------
3. SCORING ENGINE LOGIC
------------------------------------------------------------------------

*   **Initial Legitimacy Score**: 100 points.
*   **Score Deductions**: Deduct points dynamically based on the penalty value
    associated with each triggered deviation.
*   **Risk Categorization Rules**:
    - **Score 80 - 100**: LOW RISK (Priority 5) -> Green Flag. Auto-approvable.
    - **Score 50 - 79**: MEDIUM RISK (Priority 3) -> Yellow Flag. Needs reviewer eyes.
    - **Score 0 - 49**: HIGH RISK (Priority 1) -> Red Flag. Sent to top of reviewer queue.
